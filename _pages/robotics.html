ASEE Competition 2014

Indie 500

A national robotics competition held in Indianapolis, Indiana, where the objective was to autonomously navigate a scaled-down version of the Indianapolis 500 track. The model included various obstacles to enhance the difficulty of the challenge.

Quadcopter

A quadcopter I built in college. It went through two design iterations before I decided to pass it along. It never fully worked, but I had fun designing and figuring out the electronics. It also led to the creation of the drone club at MCC.

ASEE Competition 2015

Fish Derby

A national robotics competition held in Seattle, Washington, where the objective was to autonomously collect wooden fish cut-outs placed along two concentric circles on an 8x8 foot track. After collecting the fish, the robot had to deposit them into the corresponding colored bins located at the track's corners.

McLaren F1

A first-year, in-class robotics competition with multiple objectives, including track navigation and a tractor pull. The design (aesthetics and function) was left completely up to the students; I chose to loosely model mine after a McLaren F1.



❮

❯

ATtiny Swarm

A set of programmable, rechargeable robots whose functionality could be altered by adding a modular attachment called a "shield." By default, these robots would either line-follow or avoid obstacles. When a shield was attached, the base design's electronics were overridden.

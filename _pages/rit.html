Life at RIT

In the fall of 2015, I transferred from MCC to RIT, expecting a significant difference given the higher tuition. However, I quickly realized I had underestimated the value of my experience at MCC. During my first semester at RIT, many of my peers remarked on how much better prepared MCC had made them for the challenges ahead. I found MCC to be a more rewarding experience than RIT, though I respect that everyone has their own perspective. While studying at RIT, I began working full-time at Carestream and graduated in December 2016 with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering; a full year and a half ahead of schedule. I was also honored to be nominated for the Outstanding Undergraduate Scholar award, an award given only to the top 1% of students graduating from RIT.

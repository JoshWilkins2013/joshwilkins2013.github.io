Life at MCC
After graduating from high school, I enrolled at MCC in the fall of 2013. It wasn't long before I discovered their robotics competition, which I eagerly joined. That spring, our team secured 2nd place in the 2014 ASEE National Robotics Competition in Indianapolis, IN. The following year, I took on the role of secretary for the Engineering Leadership Council, where I helped organize fundraisers, coordinate trips to nearby universities, and support fellow students in the engineering program. I participated in the 2015 robotics competition, and we achieved 2nd place again. I graduated in the spring of 2015 with an associate's degree in Engineering Science.

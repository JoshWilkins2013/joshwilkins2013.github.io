Night Stands

After building a bed frame, I decided to make night stands to match the frame. This was my first time making something with dovetails and I think they came out alright. I also made a leg tapering jig which I am sure I will find use for in later projects.

❮

❯

End Tables

I want to say these are similar to my night stands, but the construction of these two projects are vastly different. I made these to complement the mission-style design of the sofa table my father made for me. They accompany the couch and provide some place for food or drink while watching TV.

❮

❯

Bar Stool

These bar stools were designed for the new bar top in my kitchen renovation. The inward leg support was a mistake as it's practically unusable as a foot rest. However, after adding a cushion to the top of these, they suffice.

❮

❯

Chest

Alright, I’ll admit it — this wasn't made by me. My father built 4 of these total, giving one to each of my brothers and I, as well as keeping one for himself.

❮

❯

Bed Frame

My first larger woodworking project was this bed frame. While sleigh-style and mission-style frames are quite nice, I wanted something more minimalistic in design and simpler to build.

❮

❯

Cutting Boards

I've made a few cutting boards, the first of which I made back in high school for my parents 25th anniversary. The latter cutting boards were made from the leftover pieces of my butcher block kitchen counter. I made three in total - one for each of my brothers and one for myself.

❮

❯

Travel Mug

This was the second thing I ever made with wood. Interestingly, I did this without a lathe.

❮

❯

Lamps

I made some lamps to go with the nightstands and bed frame I made. This design was inspired by a similar concept I found online as I thought it was neat. It has articulating joints and can be manipulated into various positions.

❮

❯

Book Case

Some place to store all the books I accumulated growing up and any books I get in the future. The walnut / cherry blend turned out better than anticipated!

❮

❯

Tissue Box

It took a couple of iterations to get the tissue lid design correct without the tissue ripping, but it works like a charm now. The lid was also designed with embedded magnets to keep it in place and allow for quick tissue box replacement.

❮

3D Tic-Tac-Toe

An interesting variation of the classical tic-tac-toe game, this one is played with 3 players in 3 dimensions!
